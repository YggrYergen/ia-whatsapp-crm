## 1. Extraction Philosophy

### What This Is NOT
- A book summary or cliff notes exercise
- An academic literature review
- A "nice quotes" collection

### What This IS
A **decision-support database**. Every extracted item must answer the question:

> "If I'm standing in front of a prospect, sitting at my desk designing an offer, or deciding how to spend the next 4 hours — does this principle change what I would do?"

If the answer is no, it doesn't make the cut.

### The Lens
Every principle is evaluated through the lens of **one specific person in one specific situation:**

- **Who:** Solo technical founder, age ~23, in Santiago, Chile
- **What:** AI WhatsApp CRM SaaS (tuAsistenteVirtual)
- **Where:** Chilean SMB market, expanding to LATAM
- **When:** 22 days to 7 clients (kill switch: May 4, 2026)
- **Resources:** No team, no funding, strong tech, weak sales muscle, 1 live client (CasaVitaCure), 1 onboarding Tuesday (fumigation)
- **Constraints:** Solo operator, limited cash runway, can't do everything at once

## 2. What Qualifies as a "Principle"

A principle is extracted if it meets **at least 2 of these 4 criteria:**

| # | Criterion | Test |
|:--|:----------|:-----|
| **C1** | **Actionable** | Can be converted to a specific action within 7 days |
| **C2** | **Counter-intuitive** | Goes against what a first-time founder would naturally do |
| **C3** | **Compounding** | Gets more powerful the more it's applied over time |
| **C4** | **Structural** | Changes the architecture of the business, not just a tactic |

### What Gets EXCLUDED
- Filler anecdotes that don't carry a transferable principle
- Principles that only apply to businesses with 50+ employees
- Motivational content without a mechanism
- Redundant restatements of the same idea (keep the sharpest version)

## 4. Impact Ranking System

Each principle gets scored on two axes:

### Axis 1: Impact Magnitude (1-5)

| Score | Label | Definition |
|:------|:------|:-----------|
| **5** | **Business-defining** | Changes the fundamental trajectory. Wrong here = business dies. |
| **4** | **Revenue-multiplying** | Directly increases revenue 2x+ or cuts costs 50%+ |
| **3** | **Significant advantage** | Creates a meaningful edge over competitors or default behavior |
| **2** | **Useful optimization** | Improves an existing process or metric by 10-30% |
| **1** | **Good to know** | Valuable context but won't change outcomes alone |

### Axis 2: Time Horizon (when it matters most)

| Code | Horizon | Timeline | Your Phase |
|:-----|:--------|:---------|:-----------|
| **H1** | Survive | Now → May 4 (22 days) | Get 7 clients or die |
| **H2** | Stabilize | May → July (60 days) | 22-25 clients, systems, legal entity |
| **H3** | Scale | July → Dec 2026 (6 months) | 50-100 clients, expansion products, possible exit |

### Combined Priority Score

```
Priority = Impact × Horizon_Weight

Where:
  H1 weight = 3  (survival — everything else is irrelevant if you don't survive)
  H2 weight = 2  (stabilization — important but you have time to iterate)
  H3 weight = 1  (scale — valuable to know now, but execution comes later)
```

This means a Score-3 principle for H1 (3×3=9) outranks a Score-5 principle for H3 (5×1=5). **Survival principles dominate.**

## 5. Extraction Process — Step by Step

### Step 1: Per-Book Deep Read (one book at a time)
- Read the full markdown file
- Extract every qualifying principle
- Tag each with: source book, chapter/section, verbatim quote (if sharp), and my plain-language restatement

### Step 2: Deduplication & Sharpening
- Merge principles that say the same thing across books (Hormozi repeats himself intentionally)
- Keep the single sharpest formulation
- Note which books reinforce the same idea (cross-references increase confidence)

### Step 3: Categorization
- Assign business area tags (1-3 per principle)
- Assign impact score (1-5)
- Assign time horizon (H1/H2/H3)
- Calculate priority score

### Step 4: Contextual Application Notes
For every principle scored ≥ 6 (priority score), add a **"For You, This Means..."** section that translates the abstract principle into a concrete action or decision for tuAsistenteVirtual. Examples:

> **Principle:** "Charge premium prices. Higher prices signal higher quality and attract better clients."
> 
> **For You, This Means:** Don't drop to 60K when a prospect pushes back. Your 80K is already 10K below the market leader. If anything, create a 120K "Pro" tier with dashboard access + priority support, and make 80K feel like the bargain.

### Step 5: The Final Output
One master document with:
1. **Executive Summary** — The 10 most critical principles for the next 22 days
2. **Full Catalog** — All principles sorted by priority score (descending)
3. **By Business Area** — Same principles grouped by area for reference
4. **Cross-Reference Matrix** — Which books reinforce which principles
5. **The "Don't" List** — Extracted anti-patterns (things the books explicitly warn against)

## 6. Quality Filters (What Makes This Not Suck)

### Filter 1: The "Monday Morning" Test
> Can you wake up Monday and DO something different because of this principle?

If a principle passes extraction but fails this test, it gets demoted to the "Good to Know" section.

### Filter 2: The "Specificity" Test
> Is this more specific than generic business advice?

"Provide value to your customers" → FAILS (everyone knows this)  
"Make your lead magnet solve one narrow, painful problem so well that the solution reveals a bigger problem only your core offer solves" → PASSES (specific mechanism)

### Filter 3: The "Already Doing It" Test
> Are you already doing this? If yes, is there a sharper version in the book?

If you're already doing it and the book doesn't add nuance, skip it. If the book reveals you're doing it WRONG or INCOMPLETELY, that's high-priority.

### Filter 4: The "Cialdini Amplifier" Test (for Pre-Suasion specifically)
> Does this psychological principle amplify or explain WHY a Hormozi tactic works?

Cialdini's work is the "why" behind Hormozi's "what." When a Cialdini principle explains the mechanism behind a Hormozi tactic, both get cross-linked and the combined insight is more valuable than either alone.
