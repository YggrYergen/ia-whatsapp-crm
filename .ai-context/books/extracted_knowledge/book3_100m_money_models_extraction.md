### MM-01: The Money Model Is a Deliberate Sequence of Offers
**Source:** Section VI, "Make Your Money Model"
**Quote:** *"A Money Model is a deliberate sequence of offers. It's what you offer, when you offer, and how you offer it to make as much money as you can as fast as you can."*

**Principle:** A Money Model is NOT just a product — it's the choreography of Attraction → Upsell → Downsell → Continuity offers, designed so that one customer's 30-day revenue covers the cost of acquiring and servicing multiple customers.

**Tags:** `MONEY` `OFFER` `SCALE`
**Impact:** 5 — Business-defining
**Horizon:** H1

Without this sequence, every prospect who says "no" to 80K walks away forever.

This gives them a reason to push through the "buyer's remorse" window. And it costs you nothing because a retained client at 65K > a churned client at 0K.

### MM-27: Gift Cards as Referral Engines

**Impact:** 3 — Significant advantage

Then you upsell them into the full 80K/month package. CasaVitaCure paid you 20K to send you a warm referral. The friend got "200K free." You got a new client for 20K CAC.

### MM-28: Say No to Say Yes — The Negative Confirmation Close
**Source:** Section III, "The Classic Upsell" — Important Notes

**Principle:** Frame your upsell as a negative question: "You don't want [X], do you?" People's trained reflex to say "no" actually means "no, I DO want X." Clever linguistic trick that increases upsell rates significantly.

**Tags:** `SALES` `PSYCH`
**Impact:** 2 — Useful optimization

**Priority:** 6

**For You, This Means:** After closing the core CRM deal: "You're not going to want the broadcast messaging feature too, are you?" or "You don't need the analytics dashboard as well, do you?" The psychological reflex makes them reconsider. Low-effort, medium-reward tactic.

### MM-30: BAMFAM — Book A Meeting From A Meeting

### MM-31: Hyper Buying Cycle — Sell More When They're Excited
**Source:** Section III, "The Classic Upsell" — Important Notes
**Quote:** *"Most buyers enter a 'hyper buying' cycle when they decide to do something new."*

**Principle:** When someone decides to start something new (new business system, new tool, new direction), they enter a brief window where they'll spend aggressively. This is the moment to offer EVERYTHING—not just the basics. If you wait, the window closes and they become conservative again.

**Tags:** `SALES` `PSYCH`
**Impact:** 3 — Significant advantage
**Horizon:** H1
**Priority:** 9

**For You, This Means:** The moment a prospect says "yes, let's do this" — that's your hyper buying window. Don't just sell the 80K CRM. Immediately offer: setup fee for priority onboarding (paid), custom AI training session (paid), WhatsApp broadcast module (paid), 6-month prepay discount. Their wallet is OPEN. If you wait until month 2 to upsell, the wallet has closed.

### MM-32: Prescription Selling > Option Selling
**Source:** Section III, "Menu Upsell"
**Quote:** *"I learned detailed and personalized instructions upsell more people than vague and general suggestions. I call this prescription upselling."*

**Principle:** Don't ask "do you want this?" — tell them "here's what you need, here's how you'll use it, here's when you take it." Write it down with specific instructions. Then ask for payment. The authority of prescription + personalization dramatically increases conversion.

**Tags:** `SALES` `PSYCH`
**Impact:** 3 — Significant advantage
**Horizon:** H1
**Priority:** 9

**For You, This Means:** Instead of "Would you also like the analytics module?", say: "Based on your business, here's what I recommend: You'll use the AI responder for all incoming WhatsApp messages. Every Monday morning, you'll check the analytics dashboard — I'll set it to auto-email you the weekly report. On the first of each month, we'll do a 30-minute review call to optimize the AI's responses. Here's the setup: [writes it out]. Want to use the payment method we have on file?" You've prescribed, not asked.

**Monday Morning Test:** ✅ Write out a "prescription plan" template for each client type (salon, medical, etc.)

### MM-33: The Economist Play — Decoy Pricing for Bundles
**Source:** Section III, "Menu Upsell" — "If You Have Two Options And Want People To Buy Both"
**Quote:** *"Present three options. Option A, Option B, and Option C (Both)…but you make the price of (C) the same as the more expensive option (B)."*

**Principle:** If you have two products and want people to buy both, add a third "bundle" option priced the same as the most expensive individual item. The bundle becomes an obvious "best deal" and drives most purchases toward the combined offering.

**Tags:** `PRICE` `PSYCH` `OFFER`
**Impact:** 3 — Significant advantage
**Horizon:** H2
**Priority:** 6

**For You, This Means:** Pricing page: A) AI Responder only: 55K/month. B) Analytics Dashboard only: 80K/month. C) AI Responder + Analytics Dashboard: 80K/month. Nobody takes B alone when C is the same price. This frames 80K as "you're basically getting analytics for free."

**Monday Morning Test:** ✅ Restructure your pricing page with 3 options using decoy logic.

### MM-34: Simple Scales, Fancy Fails
**Source:** Section VI, "Make Your Money Model" — Important Notes
**Quote:** *"It's less about having 100 products to offer, and more about having 100 ways to offer your product."*

**Principle:** Don't create new products to fill your Money Model. Create new OFFERS from the same product. Different quantities, durations, access levels, support tiers, and bundling options create dozens of offers from one core product. Complexity in products = operational nightmare. Complexity in offers = revenue multiplier.

**Tags:** `OFFER` `OPS` `SCALE`
**Impact:** 4 — Revenue-multiplying
**Horizon:** H1
**Priority:** 12

**For You, This Means:** You have ONE product: AI WhatsApp CRM. But you can create 15+ offers: Monthly/6-month/annual, Solo/Team/Enterprise tier, DIY/Setup-included/White-glove, With-analytics/Without, With-broadcasts/Without, Pay-now/Pay-later, Trial/Full. STOP building new features and START creating new OFFERS from what you have. Every "feature" is actually just an offer variation.

**Monday Morning Test:** ✅ List 10 different offers you can make from your current product without building anything new.

### MM-35: Affiliate Products Fill Money Model Gaps
**Source:** Section VI, "Make Your Money Model" — Important Notes
**Quote:** *"You can always offer somebody else's stuff in your Money Model."*

**Principle:** If you need an upsell, downsell, or continuity product but don't have one — SELL someone else's product for a commission. A dental agency sends clients to a braces manufacturer. A fitness coach recommends a supplement brand. You don't deliver the product; the affiliate does.

**Tags:** `MONEY` `SCALE` `OFFER`
**Impact:** 3 — Significant advantage
**Horizon:** H2
**Priority:** 6

**For You, This Means:** You can fill Money Model gaps with affiliate partnerships: Upsell → Partner with a professional photographer for product photos (SMBs need this for WhatsApp catalog). Downsell → DIY chatbot tool (simpler, cheaper) where you earn referral fees. Continuity → Monthly social media management from a partner agency. You earn commission, they deliver, zero extra work for you.

**Monday Morning Test:** ✅ Identify 3 complementary services you could refer clients to for commissions.

## Anti-Patterns (The "Don't" List)

| # | Anti-Pattern | Source | Why It Kills You |
|:--|:------------|:-------|:-----------------|
| **D1** | Dropping price for the same product | Feature Downsells | Erodes trust, creates word-of-mouth price erosion, makes all future prices questionable |
| **D2** | Leading with continuity as your attraction offer | Continuity Offers | Nobody commits to recurring before experiencing value. Cash-poor on Day 1. |
| **D3** | Building all four offer types at once | Make Your Money Model | Operational collapse for bootstrapped solo founders. One stage at a time. |
| **D4** | Not having ANY upsell | Classic Upsell | "You barely have a business—you have a front end." Single-offer businesses plateau instantly. |
| **D5** | Making payment plans your DEFAULT | Payment Plan Downsells | People who WOULD have paid in full take the plan → less cash upfront → can't fund growth |
| **D6** | Treating the anchor as a fake | Anchor Upsell | If you gloss over the premium, customers sense it's fake, lose trust, AND you lose money |
| **D7** | Not having a cancellation process | Continuity Discount | No exit process = 1-star reviews + lost recovery opportunities + invisible churn |
| **D8** | Discounting without getting something back | Feature Downsells | Every discount should cost the customer something: review, referral, testimonial, or feature |
